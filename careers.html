PHILOSOPHY & CAREERS

What Careers Can You Get With a Philosophy Degree?

Teacher

Whether it be as a college professor, or a high school teacher, many schools offer philosophy courses. Philosophy teachers must be good at explaining complex ideas in a way that students can understand. Teachers and professors are often good at public speaking and creating presentations. The field of teaching can suit philosophy majors who want to shape the minds of future generations.


Avg. Annual Salary of a College Profesor: $67,352

Lawyer

Many people who are interested in philosophy end up in the legal field. Philosophy majors often have traits that are highly valuable among lawyers and other legal specialists. Philosophy teaches individuals how to articulate themselves well, think logically, and respond to feedback. It is common for philosophers and philosophy students to be skilled in the art of debate. Debating ideas is a central theme to most jobs in the legal field.


Avg. Annual Salary of a Lawyer: $96,856

Psychologist

Psychology is probably the science that is most similar to the study of philosophy. Philosophy and psychology both focus on how people think. The concepts of ethics and morality are very commonly discussed in philosophies. Many philosophy majors pay special attention to acting in ethical manners. A career like social work, can allow for philosophy majors to potentially improve people's lives.


Avg. Annual Salary of a Psychologist: $105,356

Marketing

Marketing careers often require a lot of abstract thought. Philosophy majors tend to really enjoy thinking about abstract topics. In the field of marketing, employees need to be able to come up with creative ideas, and be able to communicate those ideas affectively to others. Philosophy majors tend to be creative, as well as having strong communication skills, so they would be a good fit for the marketing field.


Avg Salary of a Marketing Manager: $74,048

Journalist

Philosophy majors typically prefer opinion-based journalism, over objective reporting. Opinion-based journaling is good for philosophy majors, because it allows them to express their own ideas. Some journalists primarily communicate through writing, whereas others do so through speaking. Philosophy majors often have competencies in the areas of speaking and writing.


Avg. Annual Salary of a Journalist: $49,191

Human Resources

People who work in human resources (HR) must be able to discern what behaviors are acceptable and what behaviors are unnacceptable. Philosophy majors typically have traits that align well with these skillsets. Individuals in the field of HR must be comfortable around all sorts of different kinds of people, and in all sorts of different kinds of situations.


Avg. Annual Salary of an HR Director: $102,254
